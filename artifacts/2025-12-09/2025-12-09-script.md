This underscores Beijing's tightening grip, potentially chilling foreign investment and global perceptions of Hong Kong as a financial hub.

# Deep Dive: Border Flames
*A longstanding temple dispute explodes into airstrikes and evacuations, pitting ASEAN neighbors against each other amid dueling claims of aggression.*

**The Event**  
Fighting erupted along the Thai-Cambodian border near Preah Vihear and Ta Krabey temples, with Thai airstrikes targeting Cambodian drone and rocket sites after alleged encroachments. Kiripost details Thai shelling killing seven civilians and displacing 54,550, including prison evacuations in Oddar Meanchey. Bangkok Post counters with a Thai soldier's death and defensive strikes on invading forces. Casualties include at least eight dead overall, with schools closing and residents fleeing gunfire in provinces like Pursat and Sa Kaeo—no major territorial shifts yet, but damage to historic sites like Ta Krabey Temple.

**The Trigger/Context**  
Rooted in a 1962 ICJ ruling favoring Cambodia over Preah Vihear, tensions simmered under a fragile peace deal Thailand now rejects, citing Cambodian landmines violating accords. Kiripost frames Thai "aggression" as deflecting domestic scrutiny, while Bangkok Post accuses Cambodia of encroachment amid economic strains like China's export boom pressuring regional borders. Recent floods in Thailand and Cambodia, plus US trade talks, add pressure—Cambodia seeks donations for soldiers, while Thailand bolsters defenses, echoing 2011 clashes that killed dozens.

**The Consequence**  
ASEAN faces paralysis, with Malaysia's Anwar warning of unaffordable disputes and Trump demanding a halt, risking proxy escalations involving China (Cambodia's ally) or US influence. Global supply chains tremble: disrupted borders could spike rice prices (Thailand's key export) and hit tourism, while refugee flows strain neighbors. Japan voiced "deep concern," signaling broader Indo-Pacific ripples—if unchecked, this "slow burn" could freeze diplomacy, hobble economic recovery in Southeast Asia's $3.5T bloc, and invite external powers to meddle in regional stability.
