### Deep Dive: Border Brawl  
*A decades-old temple dispute erupts into airstrikes and evacuations, pitting ASEAN neighbors against each other amid conflicting claims of aggression and defense.*

**The Event**  
Thai forces launched airstrikes and tank assaults on Cambodian border sites, destroying casinos and military positions, while Cambodia retaliated with rockets and drones. Kiripost reported nine civilian deaths, including a child, and over 54,000 displaced; Bangkok Post confirmed Thai soldier casualties and school shutdowns. No formal war declaration, but actions breached the 2000 peace accord, with both sides trading fire near disputed temples like Preah Vihear.

**The Trigger/Context**  
Rooted in a 1962 ICJ ruling favoring Cambodia over Preah Vihear Temple, tensions flared over alleged landmines and territorial incursions. Bangkok Post accused Cambodia of fresh mine-laying, violating Ottawa Treaty norms, while Kiripost cited Thai "aggression" deflecting from domestic issues. Economic pressures, including scam syndicates in border casinos and Trump's tariff threats, add fuel—escalating a "grey zone" proxy for regional power plays amid China's influence and ASEAN's inertia.

**The Consequence**  
The clash risks ASEAN paralysis, with Cambodia withdrawing from SEA Games and Malaysia mediating amid refugee crises. Global fallout includes disrupted trade corridors, potential US intervention per Trump demands, and economic hits to tourism-dependent zones. If unchecked, it could trigger proxy wars, straining supply chains and forcing neighbors like Vietnam to pick sides in a volatile post-US election landscape.
